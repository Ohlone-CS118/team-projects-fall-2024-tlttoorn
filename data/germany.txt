Germany:

No Poverty:
Achieved: 
- In 2024, 0.34% of the population was making $3.65/day (living in extreme poverty)
Major Challenges remaining: 
- 11.60% of the population lives in poverty after taxes and transfers

Zero Hunger:
Achieved:
- 0.4% of children under 5 y/o considered underweight for their height(s)
- 2.5% of population is considered undernourished
Major Challenges Remaining:
- 20.4% of the adult population is considered obese (BMI>=30)
- More efficient use of Nitrogen (on a scale from 0-1, Germany scored 0.67)
- Exports of hazardous pesticides 

Good Health and Well-Being:
Achieved: 
- Maternal - Mortality ratio of 4.43 per 100000 live births
- Age standardized death rates due to pollution is 15 per 100000 people
- 3.3 traffic deaths per 100000 people
- Subjective health is rated as 6.79/10 (survey based)
Challenges remaining:
- 96.3% of births were attended by a skilled health personnel
- There is a 5.85 year gap in life expectancy at birth (among regions)
	
Quality Education:
Achieved: 
- The net primary school enrollment rate is 98.54%
- The participation in pre-primary school programs is 96.09% of children aged 4-6
- (Lower secondary education) Middle school completion rate is 98.7%
Challenges:
- 37.28% of the population go on to completing a degree of sorts (Higher Education)
- 29.51% of 15 y/o's are underachievers in math

Climate Action:
Achieved:
- The carbon pricing score (Euros/tCO2) on a scale of 0-100 is 73.27%
Major Challenges Remaining:
- CO2 emissions from fossil fuel exports is ~158.43 kg per capita
- CO2 emissions from fossil fuels is about 7.98 tons of CO2 per capita
- Greenhouse Gas emissions is 7.17 tCO2 per capita
